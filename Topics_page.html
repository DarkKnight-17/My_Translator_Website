Common Grammar Rules

The present perfect is a grammatical combination of the present tense and perfect aspect that is used to express a past event that has present consequences.

An article is a word that comes before a noun to show whether it's specific or general. Specific nouns use the article the and general nouns use the article a (or an, if the next word starts with a vowel sound).

A gerund is a type of verbal noun that functions as a verb in a sentence. It is formed by adding -ing to the base form of a verb, and it is often used to express an action or to describe a state of being.
